Interview Practice

Common Interview Questions for PWD Applicants

1. "Tell me about yourself"

Tip: Focus on your skills and experience, not your disability.

Example: "I'm a dedicated customer service professional with 3 years of experience in retail. I enjoy helping customers and working in team environments."

2. "What accommodations do you need?"

Tip: Be specific about accommodations that help you perform your best.

Example: "I work best with a chair that provides proper back support and occasional short breaks to maintain focus."

3. "Why do you want to work here?"

Tip: Mention the company's commitment to accessibility.

Example: "I admire your company's commitment to creating an inclusive workplace and your PWD-friendly initiatives."

Disclosure Guidance

When and How to Disclose Your Disability

During Application

Optional: You are not required to disclose during initial application.

If you choose to disclose: Focus on your abilities and how accommodations help you perform effectively.

Example: "I have a visual impairment and use screen reading software. I've successfully used this accommodation in my previous roles."

During Interview

Recommended: Discuss accommodations you might need to perform the job.

Example Script: "I want to mention that I have [disability]. With [specific accommodation], I can perform all essential job functions effectively. In my previous role, this accommodation allowed me to excel in..."

After Job Offer

Required: You must disclose if you need accommodations to perform the job.

Process: Submit accommodation request to HR with medical documentation if required. This information is kept confidential.

Sample Disclosure Statement

"I have a physical disability that requires occasional use of a wheelchair. I want to assure you that with accessible facilities and reasonable accommodations like a desk at appropriate height, I can perform all the duties of this position effectively. I have successfully worked in similar roles with these accommodations."

Philippine Republic Acts for PWD

Key Philippine Laws Protecting PWD Rights

Republic Act No. 7277 - Magna Carta for Persons with Disabilities

Also known as: Magna Carta for Disabled Persons

Key Provisions:

  • At least 1% of all government and private sector positions reserved for PWDs
  • Equal employment opportunities for qualified PWDs
  • Reasonable accommodations in the workplace
  • Tax incentives for employers hiring PWDs
  • Accessibility requirements in buildings and public transport

Effective Date: March 24, 1992

Republic Act No. 10754 - An Act Expanding the Benefits and Privileges of PWDs

Key Provisions:

  • 20% discount on medicines, medical services, and transportation
  • Value Added Tax (VAT) exemption on certain goods and services
  • 5% discount on basic necessities and prime commodities
  • Educational assistance and scholarships
  • Special discounts in restaurants, hotels, and recreational centers

Effective Date: March 23, 2016

Republic Act No. 11228 - An Act Providing for the Mandatory PhilHealth Coverage for All PWDs

Key Provisions:

  • Mandatory PhilHealth coverage for all PWDs
  • No age limit for dependent PWDs
  • Premium contributions paid by the government
  • Comprehensive health insurance benefits

Effective Date: February 22, 2019

Republic Act No. 11650 - Inclusive Education Act for Learners with Disabilities

Key Provisions:

  • Mandatory inclusive education for learners with disabilities
  • Establishment of Inclusive Learning Resource Centers
  • Training for teachers on special education
  • Accessibility in educational institutions

Effective Date: March 11, 2022

Batas Pambansa Blg. 344 - Accessibility Law

Key Provisions:

  • Architectural accessibility requirements
  • Mandatory accessibility features in public and private buildings
  • Accessible public transportation
  • Parking spaces reserved for PWDs

Effective Date: February 25, 1983

Employer Incentives

Tax Deductions for Employers:

  • Additional deduction from gross income equivalent to 25% of total salaries paid to PWD employees
  • Double deduction for expenses incurred in making the workplace accessible
  • Priority in government contracts and procurement

Your Rights as a PWD Job Seeker

Legal Protections Under Philippine Law

1. Right to Non-Discrimination

Employers cannot refuse to hire you based solely on your disability if you can perform the essential job functions with reasonable accommodations. (RA 7277)

2. Right to Reasonable Accommodations

You have the right to request accommodations that enable you to perform your job, such as modified work schedules, assistive technology, or physical modifications to the workplace. (RA 7277)

3. Right to Confidentiality

Your disability information must be kept confidential and separate from your personnel file. Only relevant information for accommodations may be shared with necessary personnel. (Data Privacy Act)

4. Right to Equal Pay

You must receive equal pay for equal work, regardless of disability. (Labor Code)

5. Protection from Harassment

You have the right to work in an environment free from harassment related to your disability. (Safe Spaces Act)

For more information or to file a complaint:

  • Dragon NaturallySpeaking - Professional speech-to-text

  • ZoomText - Screen magnifier with speech

Workplace Accommodations

  • Flexible work schedules and remote work options
  • Ergonomic workstations and adaptive equipment
  • Sign language interpreters for meetings
  • Written materials in alternative formats (Braille, large print, audio)
  • Assistive listening devices for meetings
  • Job restructuring and modified duties
  • Additional breaks for medical needs
  • Service animal accommodations
